Operations Research Algorithm Developer
Cardinal Operations
July 2021 – Present Beijing

  • End-to-End Platform : Designed an algorithmic framework integral to demand forecasting, inventory management, and supply planning platform. This involved extracting abstract elements and their interactions from over 200 real-world supply chain scenarios. These insights included production, procurement, transportation, and delivery planning. The algorithmic structure was further customized to accommodate diverse optimization objectives, constraints, rule-based heuristics, and performance evaluation.

  • Industry Solutions : Developed industry-specific optimization algorithms to address two distinct challenges: 1) Created algorithms for production planning that simultaneously maximize total demand satisfaction while minimizing both total inventory and costs in the 3C electronics sector. These algorithms supported decision-making for order commitments, capacity analysis, and material shortage assessment. 2) Designed sequencing algorithms in the automobile industry, to ensure the production sequence aligns with package and color requirements in the body shop, painting shop, and general assembly. These algorithms optimized production efficiency while adhering to critical production constraints.

  • Enterprise Applications : successful implementations of algorithms to address advanced production and scheduling (APS) challenges in two distinct contexts: 1) Streamlined planning processes for a leading passenger vehicle manufacturer across vehicle plants, engine workshops, and engine part machining. The solutions optimized production shifts, transfers, and scheduling. 2) Provided solutions for an engineer-to-order wind turbine manufacturer dealing with intricate bill of materials, enhancing their production and transportation planning. These implementations resulted in remarkable cost savings of over $7 million, attributed to reduced energy consumption, improved resource utilization, and optimized freight logistics.
