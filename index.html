Biography

I am an Operations Research Algorithm Developer at Cardinal Operations, a firm founded by Stanford PhDs in 2016 and renowned for applying Operations Research models to large companies in China. I have participated in the development of optimization algorithms in the end-to-end platform, industry-specific solutions and enterprise applications, resulting in significant cost savings.

I hold a Master’s and Bachelor’s degree in Management Science and Engineering from Renmin University of China. I have led a team on a project optimizing Sinopec’s global natural gas resources allocation and have contributed to projects on the planning of enhanced oil production and the prediction of oil well production.

I have published multiple research papers in reputable journals, including Computers & Industrial Engineering and International Journal of Production Research. These papers discusses oilfield development planning, supply chain network design, and uncertain environment portfolio selection.

Research Projects

Optimal Allocation Model for Sinopec Global Natural Gas Resources
Engineered an efficient resource allocation system for Sinopec’s global natural gas resources by developing a multi-objective procurement and production optimization model, utilizing in-depth data analysis of resource characteristics, pipe network specifics, and demand patterns.
Model and Algorithm of Enhanced Oil Production in Daqing Oilfield
Optimized a streamlined production planning process by analyzing practical requirements, which entailed defining decision variables, objectives, and constraints while also identifying potential uncertainties in the system. Engineered a production execution plan by constructing a deterministic multi-objective model, utilizing the NSGA-II based algorithm to optimize production outcomes.
Indicator Prediction Model of Adjustment Well in Shengli Oilfield
Accomplished a rigorous model evaluation for oil production prediction by implementing and comparing various predictive models, including transfer function model, SVR, and LSTM, leading to the selection of the highest-performing model.
